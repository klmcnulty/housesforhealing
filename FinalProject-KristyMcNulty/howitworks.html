How It Works

How can you receive funding?

First, when a medical need occurs, a patient whom is being treated in Abilene contacts the Area Beacon Community, or Houses For Healing directly.

In order to qualify, patients must:

  • Be treated as an outpatient with routine scheduled treatments
  • Have scheduled treatments greater than two appointments

Area Beacon Community

An Area Beacon Community is a larger community within a geographical area who helps other communities of that area. There will be an Area Beacon Community Point of Contact whom assist in facilitating care for the needs of patients administered through ministry partners. The Point of Contact person will help with communication between the Good Samaritans in Abilene and those in the patient's home area.

An Area Beacon Community is a community of communities. It should have a stronge sense of community outreach, ministerial alliances, and a point of contact for the Beacon Community. They should assist in communicating with the patient and families, with Houses For Healing, and with local churches, ministries, and outreaches.

If there is a waiting list, priority is given to those with greater need, availability of family in Abilene, and low income residents.
